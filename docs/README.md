# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to OctoAcme's project management process documentation. This folder contains standardized guides for running projects from initiation through retrospective and continuous improvement.

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a customer-first, iterative approach to project delivery with clear ownership, data-informed decisions, and psychological safety at its core. Our project lifecycle spans seven key phases:

1. **Initiation** — Validate business need, align stakeholders, and confirm go/no-go
2. **Planning** — Break work into shippable increments, identify dependencies, and establish timelines
3. **Execution & Tracking** — Manage day-to-day delivery, maintain quality standards, and track progress
4. **Risk Management & Communication** — Identify risks, maintain stakeholder alignment, and escalate proactively
5. **Release & Deployment** — Standardize releases, minimize risk, and improve observability
6. **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement** — Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements
7. **Roles & Personas** — Define responsibilities and expectations for core project team members

Each phase relies on key artifacts (one-pager, backlog, risk register), regular team rhythms (daily standups, weekly syncs, demos, retrospectives), and clear escalation paths.

### Project Management Principles

OctoAcme projects are guided by five core principles:

- **Customer-first** — Prioritize customer value and usability in every decision
- **Iterative delivery** — Deliver small, testable increments to gather feedback early
- **Clear ownership** — Each project has named Project Manager and Product Lead with explicit responsibilities
- **Data-informed decisions** — Measure impact and iterate based on evidence, not assumptions
- **Psychological safety** — Encourage feedback, learning, and blameless post-mortems to drive continuous improvement

### Team Rhythm & Cadence

OctoAcme projects maintain consistent communication and synchronization:
- **Daily standups** (15 min) — Focus on progress, blockers, and dependencies
- **Weekly delivery sync** — Show progress, flag risks, and coordinate cross-team dependencies
- **Sprint/milestone demos** — Review completed work with stakeholders
- **Monthly stakeholder updates** — Communicate status, impact, and strategic alignment
- **Retrospectives** — After each sprint, release, or milestone to capture learnings

### Key Artifacts

Core artifacts used across OctoAcme projects:
- **Project One-pager** — Problem statement, goals, success metrics, stakeholders, timeline
- **Prioritized Backlog** — Shippable features with acceptance criteria and estimates
- **Risk Register** — Identified risks with impact, likelihood, owner, and mitigation plans
- **Release Plan** — Milestones, deliverables, dependencies, and deployment schedule
- **Retrospective Notes** — Learnings and action items for continuous improvement

## How to Use This Documentation

### For Team Members
- **New to OctoAcme?** Start with [Project Management Overview](./octoacme-project-management-overview.md) to understand our approach and key roles.
- **Starting a new project?** Follow the [Project Initiation Guide](./octoacme-project-initiation.md) to validate the idea and move through our decision gates.
- **In execution?** Reference [Execution & Tracking](./octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) and [Risk Management & Communication](./octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) for team rhythm and escalation guidance.
- **Preparing a release?** Use [Release & Deployment](./octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) to follow our standardized process.
- **Retrospective time?** See [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](./octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) for structure and facilitation guidance.

### For Copilot Spaces
These process documents are designed to be added to Copilot Spaces as context. When Copilot Spaces includes this folder, it gains awareness of:
- Project lifecycle phases and when to apply them
- Key artifacts and templates for each phase
- Team roles and responsibilities
- Communication patterns and escalation paths
- Quality standards and testing requirements
- Risk management and incident response procedures
